Copper & Nickel Base Alloy

Copper-nickel base alloys, or cupronickels, are primarily composed of copper and nickel, offering excellent corrosion resistance, especially in marine environments. They maintain good mechanical properties and are easily weldable. Common applications include shipbuilding, heat exchangers, and coins. Notable alloys include C70600 (90/10) and C71500 (70/30).

    Cobalt Base Alloys

    Cobalt-base alloys are high-performance materials primarily composed of cobalt, known for exceptional wear and corrosion resistance. They maintain strength at high temperatures, making them ideal for aerospace and turbine applications. Additionally, some cobalt alloys are biocompatible, suitable for medical implants. Common uses include cutting tools, aerospace components, and medical devices.

    Ferro Alloys

    Ferroalloys are alloys of iron with other elements, primarily used to enhance the properties of steel and cast iron. They improve strength, toughness, and corrosion resistance, with common types including ferromanganese, ferrosilicon, and ferrochromium. These alloys play a crucial role in the steelmaking process, contributing to the production of various grades of steel. Their applications span construction, automotive, and manufacturing industries.
